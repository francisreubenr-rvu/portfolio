# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Technical recruiters, senior engineers, hiring managers, and potential collaborators who are evaluating Francis as an engineering candidate or collaborator. They arrive either from a job application, a GitHub link, or a referral. They are technical — they will read the tech stack, skim the project descriptions, and notice if something looks like a template.

Secondary: peers and classmates who might collaborate on future projects.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that makes a single argument in one visit: Francis Reuben R is a technically serious engineer in his first year who builds real, complete systems across AI/ML, embedded hardware, cybersecurity, and full-stack web. The portfolio is the proof. Every design decision should reinforce that argument.

Success means a recruiter or senior engineer finishes the page thinking "this is not a student project" — and either sends an email or forwards the link.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Confident. Earned.

Not aspirational or decorative. The work is the statement. The design supports the work without competing with it.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-generated portfolio layouts (hero image, three cards, contact form)
- Neobrutalism / thick borders / block shadows (already done in the HTML version)
- SaaS landing page energy: hero metrics, gradient text, glassmorphism
- Dribbble candy — visual complexity as a substitute for substance
- Student template aesthetics: centered layout, blue CTA buttons, icon + heading + text card grid
- Dark-on-dark monochrome with electric accent (the previous version of this portfolio)

## Design Principles

1. **The work speaks first.** Project cards carry more weight than hero visuals. Typography hierarchy should guide the eye toward project names and descriptions before anything decorative.
2. **Restraint as signal.** An engineer's portfolio that looks designed-not-decorated signals the same taste as their code. Every element that isn't carrying information is a candidate for removal.
3. **Ink on paper.** Light, warm, typographic. Swiss editorial sensibility: grids, hierarchy, precision. Not clinical. Not minimalist-by-default. Deliberate.
4. **No hedging.** Claims are specific (">95% accuracy", "8-frame window", "8 KB quantized model"), not vague ("built a machine learning project"). The design should have the same specificity: no generic spacing, no indifferent color choices.
5. **Earned confidence.** First-year engineer, exceptional results. The tone should reflect that: factual, not boastful. The content earns the confidence the layout projects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Reasonable color contrast on the warm paper background. No motion that can't be opted out of via prefers-reduced-motion.
